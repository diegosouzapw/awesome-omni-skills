Use this file to package creative safely and completely. Prefer current official platform documentation if there is any conflict.

## Meta

Use placement-flexible creative and expect platform adaptation across feed, stories, and reels contexts.

Checklist:

- Provide multiple high-quality source assets when possible.
- Include vertical-friendly assets for stories and reels.
- Review copy, headline, CTA, and image/video combinations for placement fit.
- Expect Meta automation to adapt presentation, but review the result rather than assuming it is always brand-safe.
- Avoid excessive text in images; while not a universal hard ban, too much text can reduce quality or delivery.

## Google Performance Max

Performance Max asset groups should be complete and theme-based.

Typical official requirements include support for:

- headlines
- long headlines
- descriptions
- images in supported orientations
- video assets
- logos
- business name
- final URL

Operator checklist:

- Group assets by product line, offer theme, or audience theme.
- Fill as many relevant asset slots as practical with strong variety.
- Include manually reviewed video whenever possible.
- Review final URL expansion and brand settings.
- Check asset group strength after packaging.
- Review generated or automatically created assets before launch.

## TikTok

TikTok creative should feel native before it feels polished.

Checklist:

- Prioritize vertical video.
- Ensure the first seconds communicate the hook clearly.
- Add readable captions.
- Review music, dubbing, and pacing for authenticity.
- Validate that Symphony-enhanced assets still match creator-native expectations.
- Export to current TikTok ad specifications before upload.

## LinkedIn

LinkedIn creative should fit a professional context.

Checklist:

- Match the chosen ad format to the objective.
- Use cleaner edits and stronger proof/value framing than short-form entertainment platforms.
- Confirm image/video dimensions and copy limits for the selected format.
- Review lead quality expectations, not just CTR.

## Packaging Rules Across Platforms

- Do not ship one master file everywhere unchanged.
- Adapt aspect ratio, opening hook, CTA framing, and copy density per platform.
- Keep a record of which source asset maps to which exported variant.
- Re-check all assets after export for cropping, caption safety area, and logo visibility.
