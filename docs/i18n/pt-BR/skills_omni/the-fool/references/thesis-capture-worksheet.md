Use this before critique when the thesis is vague, overloaded, or disputed.

## Required intake

- **Decision under review:** What is being proposed, claimed, or justified?
- **Scope:** What is in scope and out of scope?
- **Why now:** What deadline, trigger, or forcing function exists?
- **Desired outcome:** What result is the thesis trying to achieve?
- **Constraints:** Budget, time, staffing, compliance, performance, reliability, or operational limits.
- **Dependencies:** People, teams, vendors, systems, or assumptions the thesis relies on.
- **Current evidence state:** What direct evidence exists today?
- **What would change your mind?:** What result, signal, or evidence would materially alter the conclusion?

## Steelman template

> You believe that `<proposal or claim>` is the best current path because `<reason 1>`, `<reason 2>`, and `<reason 3>`, within the constraints of `<constraints>`. The decision matters because `<stakes>`. Is this a fair restatement?

## Mode selection hints

- Choose **Expose My Assumptions** when hidden premises drive the thesis.
- Choose **Test the Evidence** when confidence appears stronger than support.
- Choose **Find the Failure Modes** when execution risk is the concern.
- Choose **Attack This** when abuse, misuse, or active adversaries matter.
- Choose **Argue the Other Side** when a strong alternative case is needed.
