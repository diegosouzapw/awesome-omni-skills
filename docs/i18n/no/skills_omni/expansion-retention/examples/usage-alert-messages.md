These examples prioritize clarity and customer trust.

## 50% threshold

"You have used about half of your current allocation. No action is needed yet, but this is a good time to review recent usage trends."

## 75% threshold

"You are approaching your current plan threshold. If your current pace continues, you may exceed it before the next reset. Here are the options: stay on the current plan, review expected overage cost, or compare the next tier."

## 90% threshold

"You are close to your current plan limit. To avoid interruption or unexpected charges, please review your projected usage and choose whether to stay on the current plan, accept overage, or move to the next tier."

## Strategic-account variant

"Usage is rising faster than expected. Before any commercial change, we recommend a quick review of expected volume, billing impact, and the best-fit plan for the next period."

## Writing rules

- explain the customer impact plainly
- avoid forced urgency
- include cost visibility where possible
- include a human contact path for larger accounts
