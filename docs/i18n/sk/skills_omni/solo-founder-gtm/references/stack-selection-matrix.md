Choose tools by operating need, not brand preference.

| Function | Must-have capability | Lightweight default | Move upmarket when | Defer if |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| CRM | stage tracking, notes, tasks, searchable records | spreadsheet or lightweight CRM | records become hard to search or follow-up slips | there are fewer than ~20 active opportunities and a spreadsheet is still disciplined |
| Web analytics | traffic source and conversion tracking | lightweight web analytics | you need deeper funnel analysis or cohorting | traffic is too low to produce decisions |
| Product analytics | activation and retention events | event tool with funnel support | onboarding and retention decisions depend on product behavior | the product is not yet stable enough to instrument |
| Outreach | sequencing, suppression hygiene, reply tracking | manual founder-led email plus simple tracking | volume rises and manual follow-up breaks | messaging and ICP are still unclear |
| Enrichment | useful firmographic/contact context | manual research or minimal enrichment | targeting quality becomes the bottleneck | the founder does not yet know the target buyer well |
| Support | ticket triage, KB access, escalation | inbox plus FAQ/docs | ticket volume blocks product work | support volume is still small and highly custom |
| Automation | reliable handoffs between tools | light automations only | repeated manual steps are already understood and stable | the process is still changing every week |
| Billing | clear pricing and basic invoicing/collections | simple recurring billing | annual plans, usage billing, or more complex packaging are now proven needs | pricing is not yet settled |

## Migration Triggers

Move from a simpler setup only when one of these is true:

- follow-up quality is falling
- data is duplicated or unreliable
- the founder cannot answer basic funnel questions
- manual work repeats 3+ times per week with the same steps
- compliance, suppression, or audit needs exceed ad hoc tooling

## Anti-Patterns

- Buying enterprise tooling before repeatability exists.
- Building internal systems before off-the-shelf workflows are exhausted.
- Adding tools faster than documentation and ownership can keep up.
- Locking into annual plans before a tool is clearly mission-critical.
