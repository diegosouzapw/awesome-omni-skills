This note helps operators avoid overconfident advice. It is not legal advice.

## Core rule

Before recommending AI SDR outreach, confirm:

- target geography
- target audience type
- channels in scope
- suppression and opt-out handling
- whether internal legal/compliance review is required

## Safe guidance boundaries

You may help with:

- identifying decision points the team must resolve
- creating an internal checklist for outreach readiness
- recommending opt-out and suppression handling
- flagging when cross-border or multi-channel programs need legal review

You should not:

- declare a campaign legally compliant
- assume that B2B outreach rules are the same across jurisdictions
- treat email, SMS, LinkedIn, and phone outreach as governed by the same rules
- advise operators to ignore objections or suppression requests

## Operator questions to ask

1. Which countries or regions are being targeted?
2. Is the outreach B2B, B2C, or mixed?
3. Which channels are planned?
4. How are opt-outs and objections recorded and enforced?
5. Who owns review of regional requirements?

## Practical minimums

Recommend these minimum controls:

- documented suppression process
- clear unsubscribe or opt-out handling
- recorded ownership for complaints and objections
- CRM flagging for do-not-contact states
- escalation to legal/compliance when geography or channel risk is unclear

## Escalate immediately when

- the program spans multiple jurisdictions
- SMS or phone outreach is added without review
- the team cannot explain opt-out handling
- the team wants to automate sensitive replies after objections or complaints
- the operator asks for formal legal signoff

## Output pattern

If compliance facts are incomplete, say:

- what is known
- what is unknown
- why launch advice should stay conditional
- what must be reviewed before scaling

That keeps the workflow useful without pretending to provide legal advice.
