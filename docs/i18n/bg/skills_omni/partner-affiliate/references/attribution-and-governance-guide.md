This guide defines the operating objects that reduce disputes.

## Core definitions

- **Referral:** A partner-generated introduction or tracked visit that meets minimum documented conditions.
- **Qualified referral:** A referral that matches the ICP and passes explicit acceptance criteria.
- **Accepted lead:** A lead accepted by the receiving commercial owner under documented rules.
- **Registered deal:** An opportunity formally recorded and protected for a partner under the registration policy.
- **Partner-sourced:** The partner originated the opportunity or materially created the buying process according to policy.
- **Partner-influenced:** The partner materially affected progression or close, but did not originate the opportunity.
- **Payable conversion:** The exact event that triggers payout eligibility.
- **Reversal event:** A documented reason payment is reduced, delayed, or clawed back.

## Minimum policy decisions

1. What counts as a valid referral?
2. What counts as accepted?
3. When does registration begin?
4. How long does account or deal protection last?
5. How are direct-sales overlaps resolved?
6. What data fields are authoritative in a dispute?
7. Who can approve exceptions?
8. How quickly must disputes be reviewed?

## Evidence sources for disputes

- CRM timestamps and ownership history
- Partner-platform referral IDs or links
- Meeting history and introductions
- Opportunity creation dates
- Account mapping snapshots
- Manual notes approved by named owners

## Practical rule

If sourced and influenced revenue are both meaningful, track both. Do not force one metric to do both jobs.

## Review cadence

- Monthly: dispute count, reversal rate, missing-data rate
- Quarterly: policy exceptions, conflict patterns, source-quality trends
- Semiannual: whether payout logic still fits unit economics
