Use this guide to choose the correct partner motion before discussing tools, recruitment volume, or commission rates.

## Quick selection map

| Context | Usually start with | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Low ACV, self-serve, broad creator/blog audience | Affiliate | Efficient for scalable reach if tracking and compliance are controlled |
| Warm-intro ecosystem of customers, consultants, agencies, or advisors | Referral | Lower operational overhead than a full affiliate network |
| Strong workflow adjacency and shared accounts | Integration partner | Product fit and joint customer value matter more than pure lead volume |
| Procurement-heavy enterprise buying motion | Reseller / channel / co-sell | Better fit for account coverage and commercial navigation |
| Need cloud or app ecosystem distribution | Marketplace / strategic partner | Useful when buyers expect procurement through an existing marketplace |
| Experimental joint demand generation | Co-marketing pilot | Good for testing GTM fit before deeper investment |

## Decision questions

1. Is the product sold self-serve, sales-led, or hybrid?
2. What is the ACV or expected customer value?
3. Is the buyer journey influenced more by creators/content, consultants/advisors, product integrations, or procurement partners?
4. Is success measured in clicks, qualified leads, sourced revenue, influenced pipeline, product adoption, or market access?
5. Can the business support one-time payouts, recurring revenue share, or discounted resale margins?
6. Is there meaningful account overlap or workflow adjacency with potential partners?
7. Will channel conflict with direct sales be a major issue?

## Warning signs

- If you cannot define a payable event, the program is not ready.
- If you cannot explain why a partner type should care, recruitment will underperform.
- If the product has complex implementation requirements, a pure affiliate model may attract low-fit traffic.
- If direct sales owns complex enterprise accounts, channel rules must exist before broad recruitment.

## Model notes

### Affiliate
Best for scalable acquisition where the partner mostly promotes rather than sells or integrates.

### Referral
Best for trusted introductions from existing relationships. Operationally simpler than affiliate when volume is lower and relationship quality is higher.

### Integration partner
Best when joint customer value and product fit can create retention, adoption, or sourced opportunities.

### Reseller / channel / co-sell
Best when partners help navigate procurement, implementation, or account coverage.

### Marketplace
Best when buyers already transact or discover solutions through a platform ecosystem.

### Co-marketing
Best as a test motion, not a substitute for attribution and ownership discipline.
