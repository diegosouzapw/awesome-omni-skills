Use this worksheet to compare likely motions before recommending one.

## Inputs to collect

- Product type
- Primary user
- Primary buyer
- Typical ACV or price band
- Time to first value potential
- Setup and integration burden
- Security or procurement friction
- Number of stakeholders involved in purchase
- Natural expansion path
- Team capacity for sales and customer success

## Motion options

| Motion | Best fit signals | Weak fit signals | Typical sales role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Self-serve PLG | Fast standalone value, low friction, user-led adoption, manageable marginal cost | Long setup, enterprise review, many stakeholders | Minimal or reactive |
| Hybrid / PQL-led | Users can start alone, but account growth creates buying signals | Weak product usage signals, unclear sales value-add | Sales enters after usage-based qualification |
| Sales-assisted PLG | Try-before-buy works, but enterprise packaging or rollout needs support | Product cannot show value without heavy guidance | Sales helps expansion, procurement, and rollout |
| Sales-led | High ACV, many stakeholders, heavy review, guided proof required | Product clearly sells itself in minutes | Sales leads discovery, demo, and close |

## Evidence scoring grid

Score each item as:

- `+1` strong fit
- `0` mixed or unknown
- `-1` weak fit

## Recommendation rule

Do not rely on the total score alone.

Use the scores to support a narrative recommendation:

1. Name the best-fit motion by segment.
2. Name the strongest reason it fits.
3. Name the main risk or assumption.
4. Explain why the main alternatives were rejected.

## Output prompt

Use this wording in your response:

- **Recommended motion:**
- **Why this fits:**
- **Why not the main alternative:**
- **Assumptions:**
- **Confidence level:** low / medium / high
- **Next test:**
