Use this playbook when changing existing pricing. The goal is to improve economics without creating avoidable churn, confusion, or contract risk.

## 1. Confirm why pricing is changing

Common reasons:
- low gross margin
- wrong charge metric
- poor enterprise fit
- bill shock complaints
- weak expansion under flat pricing
- provider-cost changes

State the primary reason clearly before changing packaging.

## 2. Review constraints first

Check:
- current contracts and notice terms
- annual commitments
- discount sprawl
- channel or reseller dependencies
- customer cohorts likely to be hurt most

Do not recommend migration until these are understood.

## 3. Choose the migration shape

| Migration type | When to use |
| --- | --- |
| Grandfather existing customers | When trust or contract stability matters more than short-term margin |
| New pricing for new customers only | When testing a new model safely |
| Cohort rollout | When you want controlled learning before full launch |
| Forced migration | Only when contracts, economics, and communication are strong enough |

## 4. Define success metrics

Track at minimum:
- conversion or win rate
- activation
- expansion
- gross margin
- churn / retention
- support tickets related to pricing confusion

## 5. Prepare communication

Explain:
- what is changing
- why it is changing
- what is included
- who is affected and when
- how customers can estimate future spend
- what protections or grandfathering apply

## 6. Roll out in phases

Suggested sequence:
1. internal alignment and approval
2. customer cohort selection
3. sales and support enablement
4. pilot rollout
5. metric review
6. broader rollout or revision

## 7. Watch for failure modes

| Failure mode | Likely cause | Response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Margin improves but conversion drops | package too complex or expensive | simplify packaging, increase included usage, revisit entry tier |
| Customers complain about surprise bills | weak transparency | add alerts, caps, clearer language, better dashboards |
| Enterprise deals stall | procurement mismatch | add annual commits, custom packaging, or enterprise-only options |
| Heavy users stay unprofitable | overages too low or included usage too high | tighten limits, introduce minimums, review cohort economics |
