Use these labels in synthesis. Confidence must reflect evidence quality and unresolved uncertainty, not tone.

## HIGH

Use only when:
- the main claims are supported by mostly direct evidence
- key assumptions are explicit and reasonable
- major failure paths have been examined
- unresolved risks are limited and bounded

## MEDIUM

Use when:
- the thesis is plausible but key parts still rely on indirect evidence
- one or two material assumptions remain unresolved
- failure modes are understood but not fully tested

## LOW

Use when:
- important claims rely on anecdote, assertion, or partial evidence
- major assumptions remain unvalidated
- consequences of being wrong are meaningful

## PIVOT

Use when:
- the current thesis is materially undermined
- a core assumption fails
- available evidence points away from the proposal
- proceeding without reframing would be irresponsible

## Calibration rules

- Never use `HIGH` to reward rhetorical coherence.
- If evidence quality is weak, confidence must fall even if the argument sounds polished.
- If the user answers objections well, confidence may improve, but only if the answers add real support or reduce uncertainty.
- Prefer `LOW` over unjustified certainty.
