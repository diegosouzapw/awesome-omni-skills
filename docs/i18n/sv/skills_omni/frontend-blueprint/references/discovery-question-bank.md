Use these prompts selectively. Do not ask all of them at once.

## Small Requests

Use 1-2 questions:

- What should this component help the user do?
- Do you have a screenshot or product reference you want it to feel like?
- What should stay the same, and what should change?
- Is this component used on mobile, desktop, or both?

## Medium Requests

Use a short structured pass:

- What are we building?
- Who is it for?
- What is the main action or outcome on this screen?
- Are there existing brand, product, or component-library constraints?
- Can you share 2-3 references and say what you like in each?
- Anything you definitely do not want?

## Large Requests

Go deeper:

- What are the main screens or flows?
- What does success look like for the user?
- Is there an existing design system or token set?
- What accessibility or compliance constraints matter here?
- Will the UI appear in multiple responsive contexts or embedded containers?
- Do we need dark mode, localization, or dense data presentation?

## If the User Has No References

Offer contrast pairs:

- spacious and editorial vs dense and operational
- soft and friendly vs sharp and technical
- neutral and restrained vs colorful and expressive
- card-based and modular vs document-like and continuous

Then ask:

- Which direction is closer?
- What definitely feels wrong?
- Should this feel more premium, more utilitarian, or more approachable?

## Screenshot Interpretation Questions

When the user shares a screenshot:

- What do you want to preserve from this?
- What feels wrong today: spacing, hierarchy, color, typography, density, or interaction?
- Is this representative of the whole product or just one weak area?
- Do we need to stay visually compatible with adjacent screens?

## Approval Check

Before code, confirm:

- user goal is clear
- visual direction is concrete
- constraints are known
- responsive expectations are known
- no major disagreement remains
