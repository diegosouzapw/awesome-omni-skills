Use this intake before recommending AI pricing.

## Product and buyer

1. What kind of product is this?
   - copilot
   - agent
   - API / platform
   - AI-enabled service

2. Who is the buyer?
   - developer
   - business user
   - team manager
   - operations leader
   - enterprise procurement / IT

3. What is the sales motion?
   - self-serve
   - sales-assisted
   - enterprise

## Current state

4. Do you already have pricing? If yes, what model?
5. Are you pricing for new customers, repricing existing ones, or both?
6. Are there contracts, annual deals, or discounts that limit changes?

## Value and packaging

7. What visible value does the customer buy?
   - productivity for a user
   - tasks completed
   - outcomes achieved
   - deliverables produced
   - infrastructure access

8. What unit would customers understand most easily on an invoice?
9. What are the top 1-3 features or workflows that drive willingness to pay?

## Cost and usage

10. Which AI provider(s) and model(s) are used today?
11. What is average usage per customer or task?
12. What does p95 usage look like?
13. What is the retry/error rate?
14. Is there human review, escalation, or support labor in the loop?
15. Are there caching, batching, or routing optimizations already in place?

## Market and positioning

16. What do customers compare you to today?
17. Is competitor pricing shaping buyer expectations?
18. Is the goal adoption, expansion, enterprise dealability, or margin improvement?

## Enterprise and governance

19. Are buyers asking for BYOK, private deployment, or procurement-specific terms?
20. Are spend predictability, caps, or annual commits important to closing deals?

## Output checklist

Do not recommend pricing until you can state:
- product archetype
- buyer
- external billable metric
- internal cost meter
- margin risks
- need for migration or BYOK review
