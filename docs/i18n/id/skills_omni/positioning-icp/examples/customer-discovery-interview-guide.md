Use neutral prompts. Do not lead the interviewee toward your preferred answer.

## Best-customer interview prompts

- What was happening in your team before you started looking for a solution?
- What problem felt urgent enough to act on?
- What alternatives did you consider, including doing nothing?
- Why did those alternatives fall short?
- Who else was involved in the decision?
- What would have made you not buy?
- What changed after adoption?
- What would you miss most if the product disappeared?

## Lost-deal prompts

- What outcome were you trying to achieve?
- What caused you to choose another option or delay the decision?
- What concerns did stakeholders raise?
- Was the issue fit, timing, proof, budget, or internal priority?

## Messaging extraction prompts

- What words would you use to explain the problem to a peer?
- Which phrase in our story felt most clear or most confusing?
- What claim felt hard to believe?
- What outcome would be worth forwarding to your manager or CFO?

## Interview hygiene

- ask about past behavior, not hypotheticals when possible
- separate what mattered to the user from what mattered to the buyer
- capture exact phrases, not paraphrases only
- label each finding with confidence and source
